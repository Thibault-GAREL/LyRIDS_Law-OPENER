\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Datasets}
\label{sec:exp:datasets}
We evaluate on three public legal NER datasets that, together, separate two kinds of distribution shift. \textbf{E-NER} \citep{au2022ener} -- annotated U.S.\ SEC/EDGAR filings -- is in \emph{English}, so it isolates a \emph{pure domain shift} from the general-domain data the embedder was trained on. \textbf{LeNER-Br} \citep{luzdearaujo2018lenerbr} (Brazilian court decisions, Portuguese) and \textbf{German-LER} \citep{leitner2020germanler} (German federal court decisions) add a \emph{cross-lingual} shift on top of the domain shift, stressing an English-centric embedder well outside its training distribution. \autoref{tab:legal-datasets} summarises them with the entity-type distribution of their test mentions; all three exhibit a strongly skewed, long-tailed inventory typical of legal text (one or two dominant types and several rare, schema-specific ones).

\subsection{Setup}
\label{sec:exp:setup}
Embedder, detector and heads are exactly those of the general-domain OPENER (\autoref{sec:method}); the entity embedder is loaded from the Hugging Face Hub and is \emph{not} retrained on legal data.
