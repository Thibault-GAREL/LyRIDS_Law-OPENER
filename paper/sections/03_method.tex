\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

We apply OPENER \emph{as is} to the legal domain: the pipeline, the detector and the entity embedder are reused unchanged from the general-domain system, and only the lightweight typing head is fit (or run zero-shot) on the legal targets. No component is retrained on legal data, which lets us measure how well a frugal, general-purpose open-world pipeline \emph{transfers} to legal NER.

\subsection{Mention detection and embedding}
Spans are proposed by a frozen GLiNER detector \citep{zaratiana2024gliner} (GLiNER-M for OPENER-Sup, GLiNER-L for OPENER-ZS; \autoref{sec:exp:setup}) and embedded \emph{in context} by Nomic v1.5 \citep{nussbaum2024nomic}, a Matryoshka-truncatable sentence embedder \citep{kusupati2022matryoshka} that we reuse from the general-domain OPENER \citep{anon2026opener}, where it was sharpened by a triplet contrastive step on English, general-domain CoNLL-2003 \citep{tjongkimsang2003conll} spans and an error-driven hard-negative mining pass. Nothing here is legal-specific: the embedding space was never shown a legal type, which is exactly the transfer this paper tests. The embedder is released on the Hugging Face Hub and loaded as-is; no legal-domain fine-tuning is performed.

\subsection{Entity typing}
\paragraph{OPENER-ZS} The zero-shot head, the open-world default, uses no target labels: each legal type $t$ is represented by the embedding of its name (a label-name prototype); a mention is assigned the nearest prototype by cosine similarity, refined by a transductive spherical $k$-means over the unlabelled test mentions, and \emph{fused} with the detector's own zero-shot label,
\begin{equation}
s_t(i)=\cos(e_i,p_t)+\beta\,g_i\,\mathbb{1}\!\left[t=\ell_{\text{GLiNER}}(i)\right],
\end{equation}
with $\beta{=}0.05$.

\paragraph{OPENER-Sup} A balanced linear classifier (LinearSVC, \texttt{class\_weight=balanced} \citep{pedregosa2011scikit,fan2008liblinear}) is fit on the legal target's training labels: rather than a single label-name match, it carves the embedding space into one region per type, separating types whose vectors sit close together as long as their regions do not overlap. This is the supervised upper bound; it needs target labels.

\subsection{Evaluation}
We report both \emph{typing-on-gold} (perfect detection) and \emph{end-to-end} protocols, aligning predicted and gold mentions by character offsets with sentinel labels for detection errors, following the general-domain setup. Quality is the Adjusted Mutual Information (AMI) \cite{vinh2010ami}; we also report per-sentence latency (p50) and energy (Wh, measured with CodeCarbon \cite{lacoste2019codecarbon}).
