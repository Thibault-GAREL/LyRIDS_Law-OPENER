\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

We apply \textbf{OPENER} \emph{as is} to the legal domain: the pipeline, the detector and the entity embedder are reused unchanged from the general-domain system, and only the lightweight typing head is fit (or run zero-shot) on the legal targets. No component is retrained on legal data, which lets us measure how well a frugal, general-purpose open-world pipeline \emph{transfers} to legal NER.

\subsection{Mention detection and embedding}
Spans are proposed by a frozen \textbf{GLiNER} detector \cite{zaratiana2024gliner} (GLiNER-M for OPENER-Sup, GLiNER-L for OPENER-ZS; \autoref{sec:exp:setup}) and embedded \emph{in context} by \textbf{Nomic v1.5} \cite{nussbaum2024nomic}, a Matryoshka-truncatable sentence embedder \cite{kusupati2022matryoshka} that we reuse from the general-domain OPENER, where it was sharpened by a triplet contrastive step and an error-driven hard-negative mining pass. The embedder is released on the Hugging Face Hub and loaded as-is; no legal-domain fine-tuning is performed.

\subsection{Entity typing}
\paragraph{OPENER-Sup} A balanced linear classifier (LinearSVC, \texttt{class\_weight=balanced} \cite{fan2008liblinear}) is fit on the legal target's training labels. This is the supervised upper bound; it needs target labels.

\paragraph{OPENER-ZS} The zero-shot head uses no target labels: each legal type $t$ is represented by the embedding of its name (a label-name prototype); a mention is assigned the nearest prototype by cosine similarity, refined by a transductive spherical $k$-means over the unlabelled test mentions, and \emph{fused} with the detector's own zero-shot label,
\begin{equation}
s_t(i)=\cos(e_i,p_t)+\beta\,g_i\,\mathbb{1}\!\left[t=\ell_{\text{GLiNER}}(i)\right],
\end{equation}
with $\beta{=}0.05$.

\subsection{Evaluation}
We report both \emph{typing-on-gold} (perfect detection) and \emph{end-to-end} protocols, aligning predicted and gold mentions by character offsets with sentinel labels for detection errors, following the general-domain setup. Quality is the Adjusted Mutual Information (AMI) \cite{vinh2010ami}; we also report per-sentence latency (p50) and energy (Wh, measured with CodeCarbon \cite{lacoste2019codecarbon}).
